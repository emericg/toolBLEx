# Interesting Frequency Bands: below 2200 MHz


#### 🔴 Emergency & Safety Services

| Band              | Frequency         | Notes                                 |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AM Broadcast      | 530–1700 kHz      | Strong local stations                 |
| Aviation NDB      | 190–535 kHz       | Non-directional beacons               |
| Marine distress   | 156.8 MHz (Ch 16) | International calling/distress channel |
| Aviation VHF      | 118–137 MHz       | ATC, pilot comms (AM mode)            |
| PMR446            | 446.0–446.2 MHz   | Handheld walkie-talkies (EU)          |
| TETRA (emergency) | 380–400 MHz       | Police/fire in many EU countries      |


#### 📡 Broadcast

| Band              | Frequency         | Notes                                 |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FM Broadcast      | 87.5–108 MHz      | Strong local stations                 |
| DAB+              | 174–240 MHz (Band III) | Digital radio (Europe)           |
| Longwave AM       | 148.5–283.5 kHz   | Low frequency broadcast               |

#### ⭐ Top Picks for a First Session

- **FM Broadcast @ 87.5–108 MHz** — strong signals, known frequencies, great for calibration
- **AM Broadcast @ 530–1700 kHz** — strong signals, great for calibration
- **ADS-B @ 1090 MHz** — constant aircraft transponder traffic, almost anywhere
- **ISM 433 MHz** — tons of random IoT activity (remotes, sensors, ...)
- **ISM 868 MHz** — tons of random IoT activity (remotes, sensors, ...)
